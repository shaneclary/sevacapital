Executive Summary: The Path Forward for Seva

Core Recommendation

To maximize its global impact, the Seva Foundation should pursue a phased, data-driven expansion focused on scaling its proven, cost-effective models in high-need regions. The most successful path forward involves a dual strategy: first, deepening impact in established programs in India and Guatemala by scaling the Vision Center and Global Sight Initiative models; and second, strategically expanding into high-potential areas in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.

Proven Track Record

Seva has nearly 50 years of experience building sustainable, locally-led programs with scientific rigor and cultural sensitivity245.

Tracking 120+ performance indicators across 20+ countries 231 265

Vision Center Impact

Each Vision Center creates immediate access to eye care for 50,000-500,000 people, treating 3,600 patients annually258.

170 VCs established across 12 countries in 5 years 243

This expansion will be supported by integrating new technologies like mobile health and AI diagnostics, diversifying funding through corporate and high-net-worth donor partnerships, and strengthening the "hub-and-spoke" model with local hospitals. With a projected five-year budget of $153.3 million, this strategy can leverage Seva's exceptional $1:$36 return on investment to generate over $5.5 billion in social and economic benefits, restoring sight to millions and building sustainable eye care systems worldwide.

Economic Impact Analysis

Seva's research shows that vision loss robs low- and middle-income countries of an estimated $1 trillion in productivity each year 468. Simple interventions like a $6 pair of glasses or $50 cataract surgery can unlock decades of opportunity463.

Children who receive glasses in grade school can earn up to 78% more over their lifetime 458. The World Bank recognizes cataract surgery as one of the most cost-effective health interventions globally485.
